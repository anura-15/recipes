Recipe for authentic Neapolitan Pizza

Description of the dish

What makes neapolitan pizza different? First of all, the crust is really thin, but the crust should be nice and thick.

Secondly, the dough is made by hand and fermented for atleast 24 hours, giving it sourdough like texture and flavour. Still, it only contains four ingredients!

A classic pizza from Napoli is only topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, and olive oil. Parmesan is still allowed in my book though.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 640g of 00 Flour
  • 360g of room temperature water
  • 14g of fine sea salt
  • 0.5g of instant yeast or 1.5g of fresh yeast

For the toppings

  • 300g of tinned San Marzano tomatos
  • 15g of tomato puree
  • sprinkling of sea salt
  • 30g of parmesan
  • Also:
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 250g of fresh mozzarella
  • a few glugs of a good quality olive oil
  • a handful of fresh basil leaves

Method

For the dough

  1. Add the dry ingredients into a large bowl and mix thoroughly.
  2. Start to add water until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Cover the bowl with a dry tea towel or cloth.
  4. Leave the dough for about one hour.
  5. Knead the dough on the counter for five minutes.
  6. Place the dough back into the bowl and cover tightly. Leave it for around 20 hours at room temperature. This is called proofing.
  7. Cut the dough into four roughly equal parts.
  8. Put the dough balls into a small box and cover. Leave it again for 4-6 hours.

For the sauce

  1. Blend the tin of tomatos into a smooth sauce using a blender.
  2. Cook it until it reaches the desired thickness.
  3. Add the tomato paste.
  4. Season to taste with salt and pepper.

For the shaping and baking

  1. Make sure your pizza steel or baking tray is on the oven .
  2. Get your oven as hot as possible.
  3. Dust the dough ball with plenty of flour, and place it into a floured bowl.
  4. Press the dough from the center towards the edges. You essentially want to move air from the middle of the dough to the outside.
  5. Keep turning the dough while you work the air towards the edges. It should begin to resemble a pizza.
  6. Place the dough on the counter and stretch it.
  7. Repeat until the dough has a diameter of 20-25cm.
  8. Top the pizza with two soup spoons of sauce.
  9. Spread half your mozarella evenly across it. You could add olive oil or parmesan now.
  10. The baking time depends on the heat of your oven, anywhere from 90 seconds to 8 minutes.
  11. When ready, remove it from the oven and sprinkle parmesan.
  12. Finish with olive oil, basil and black pepper.
  13. Enjoy!
